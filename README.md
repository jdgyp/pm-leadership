# PM Leadership Framework

> A practical methodology for leading product teams with clarity, impact, and alignment.

---

## Overview

Effective product leadership isn't just about shipping features — it's about creating the conditions for great product thinking, enabling the right decisions at the right time, and building teams that continuously improve. This repo captures a leadership approach built on three interdependent pillars, underpinned by a consistent set of principles.

## The Three Pillars

### 1. Set Up for Success
The first responsibility of a product leader is to remove ambiguity. Teams that lack context, clear goals, or role clarity burn time on the wrong things. This pillar covers how to establish the foundation for a high-performing product function — from team structure and onboarding, to goal-setting and stakeholder maps.

→ [Full framework: Set Up for Success](01-frameworks/01-set-up-for-success.md)

### 2. Driving Right Behaviours
Strategy dies in execution. This pillar focuses on the habits, norms, and accountability structures that keep a team moving in the right direction — not through micromanagement, but through creating an environment where the right behaviours emerge naturally.

→ [Full framework: Driving Right Behaviours](01-frameworks/02-driving-right-behaviours.md)

### 3. Working & Decision-Making Frameworks
Product teams face a constant stream of decisions under uncertainty. This pillar captures the repeatable frameworks used to prioritise, trade off, and move forward with confidence — even when the data is incomplete.

→ [Full framework: Working & Decision-Making Frameworks](01-frameworks/03-decision-making-frameworks.md)

## Core Principles

These three principles run through every pillar and every decision:

**Outcome-Driven**
We optimise for outcomes, not output. Every piece of work should trace back to a measurable change in user or business behaviour. Features are a means to an end.

**Customer-First**
Decisions start with the customer. Before asking "what should we build?", we ask "what problem are we solving, and for whom?" Qualitative and quantitative signals are both treated as first-class inputs.

**Cross-Functional Alignment & Influence**
Product leadership is largely an exercise in leading without authority. This means investing in relationships, communicating clearly across different languages (engineering, design, commercial), and creating shared ownership of outcomes.
