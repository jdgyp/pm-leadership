# Outcome-Based Roadmap Template

> A roadmap is not a delivery schedule. It's a statement of intent: here are the outcomes we're pursuing, here's why, and here's roughly how we plan to get there.

---

## Roadmap Principles

Before filling in the template, align the team on these principles:

1. **Outcomes, not features.** The rows are problems to solve, not features to ship.
2. **Confidence, not certainty.** Further out = lower confidence. That's fine — make it visible.
3. **Living document.** A roadmap should be reviewed and updated every 4–6 weeks, not once a year.
4. **Explain the why.** Every theme on the roadmap should trace back to a strategic goal.

## The Roadmap

### Time Horizon Guide

- **Now** (current cycle): High confidence. Committed. Detailed requirements exist or are in progress.
- **Next** (1–2 cycles out): Medium confidence. Problem is well-defined; solution direction is known.
- **Later** (3+ cycles out): Low confidence. Direction only. May change significantly.

## What's Not on This Roadmap (and Why)

Document explicitly what was considered and deprioritised. This builds trust with stakeholders and prevents scope creep from re-introducing deprioritised work.

| Initiative | Why Deprioritised | When to Revisit |
|---|---|---|
| _Mobile app_ | _Insufficient demand signal; web covers 90% of use cases today_ | _When mobile sessions exceed 30% of traffic_ |
| _Enterprise SSO_ | _Only 3 enterprise leads in pipeline; not a blocker yet_ | _When enterprise pipeline grows meaningfully_ |

## Review Cadence

| Audience | Frequency | Format |
|---|---|---|
| Product team | Every cycle | Live review in planning |
| Engineering lead | Fortnightly | Async + brief sync |
| Executive / stakeholders | Monthly | Written update + optional Q&A |

---

*Roadmap guidance: Resist the urge to fill in the "Later" column with false precision. The further out you commit, the more you're guessing. Make your confidence levels honest and visible — stakeholders will trust you more for it.*
